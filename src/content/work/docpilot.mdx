## TL;DR

An AI document agent for frontline vehicle-loan officers — built as a PM take-home for Superleap (AI-native CRM). It automates the manual WhatsApp document-collection loop. Designed to take an officer from ~4–5 to ~8–10 files/day. Shipped as a live interactive prototype, not a deck.

---

## Context

Brief was "design an AI CRM feature that gives frontline users time back." I picked BFSI → digital vehicle lending and the loan officer as the user.

The chokepoint: requesting docs via WhatsApp, waiting on piecemeal uploads, chasing borrowers, manually typing each document into the CRM, eyeballing for errors. This caps a good officer at ~4–5 files/day. The fix isn't more features — it's removing the wait-and-chase loop.

I interviewed an actual frontline vehicle-loan officer at IDFC to ground the design in real workflow, not assumptions.

---

## The calls I made

### 1. Grounded it in primary research, not assumptions

Interviewed a frontline vehicle-loan officer at IDFC before scoping a single feature. The core feature — "auto-tell the customer what's missing or wrong without human involvement" — is literally what he asked for. Tradeoff: a narrower, validated scope over a broader impressive-sounding feature set.

### 2. Scoped by deciding what NOT to build

DocPilot owns only the manual KYC/income doc layer. It deliberately does **not** rebuild Anumati (the RBI-regulated Account Aggregator that already auto-pulls bank data post-OTP) and does **not** touch pricing/EMI.

The research showed drop-off happens at pricing — but the officer's pain is time. So I targeted officer time, not the flashier drop-off metric, and said so explicitly. Non-goals are the senior move.

### 3. Decided where not to apply AI

DocPilot automates grunt work but the human owns the trust moments. It never auto-rejects. It escalates name mismatches for one-click officer review. It leaves the fraud-sensitive Anumati OTP/consent call to the human. Knowing where to stop is the product decision.

### 4. Made the proposal a working prototype, not a deck

The build presents research → problem → solution and demonstrates the feature live: synced CRM + WhatsApp panels, four realistic applicant scenarios. Show, don't tell.

## Outcome

- Live interactive prototype at `superleap-docpilot.netlify.app`
- Four end-to-end scenarios: name mismatch, blurry photo, expired GST, RC ownership anomaly
- Designed for ~2x officer throughput (4–5 → 8–10 files/day)
