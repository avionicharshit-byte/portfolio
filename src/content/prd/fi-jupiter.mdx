## TL;DR

**Product:** Jupiter — neobank account opening / EKYC flow.  
**Who:** Urban users aged 18–35, primarily mobile-first, opening a zero-balance digital savings account.  
**Why now:** User research (n=10) shows 100% of respondents want a fully online process, and 100% are willing to spend less than 5 minutes. The current flow exceeds that. Every extra step is measurable drop-off.  
**Success metric:** Account-opening completion rate (start → funded account). Target: +15pp in 90 days.  
**Launch:** Phased, with a 30-day decision gate.

---

## Background & Problem

**First-hand evidence:** In March 2024, my Fi app access was banned because the AI-powered KYC verification failed — likely due to a noisy environment during the process. As a result, I couldn't call customer care from the same device. After reaching out to Fi's customer care from a separate device (unresolved), I had to contact one of Fi's PMs directly on LinkedIn. The issue was resolved after a week. A typical user would have churned at step one of that recovery path.

This isn't a fringe case. It's the product's failure mode: a process with no graceful fallback for AI failures.

Jupiter operates on Federal Bank's regulated rails. The statutory KYC requirements — PAN, Aadhaar OTP, facial verify, signature — are fixed. The problem is not removing steps; it's removing redundancy and perceived friction within those steps, and building a recovery path when AI fails.

**Competitive context:** Fi and Jupiter lead on AI KYC and UX simplicity vs. HDFC, ICICI, and Kotak (which require in-person or branch steps). The gap isn't Fi vs. Jupiter — it's that both have headroom versus their own stated positioning as "effortless digital banking."

**User research (n=10):** 100% wanted a fully online process. 100% willing to spend under 5 minutes. Younger cohort (18–25) had the fewest issues — older users struggled more with verification steps.

## Goals & Non-Goals

**Goals**
- Consolidate redundant OTPs into one verification pass
- Pre-fill PAN + Aadhaar fields from retrieval (eliminate re-entry)
- Manual video KYC as a graceful fallback when AI KYC fails
- Single consolidated review screen before submit
- Instrument per-step drop-off (currently a black box)

**Non-Goals**
- Removing any statutory KYC step
- Changing fraud/ML check logic or reducing fraud flag rate
- Redesigning post-onboarding home or investing flows
- Building a concierge service (high effort, 3% use-case)
- Targeting non-urban or non-mobile-first segments (separate KYC path)

## User Stories / JTBD

- *When I start opening an account, I want to finish in under 5 minutes so I don't abandon mid-way.*
- *When I enter my PAN, I want my details auto-filled so I don't retype what the system already knows.*
- *When KYC fails, I want a clear fallback so I'm not locked out of my own account.*
- *When I've verified everything, I want to see one clear summary so I know what I'm submitting.*

## Functional Requirements

**P0 — Must ship**
- Consolidate 3 separate OTP requests into one verification pass (phone, email, Aadhaar)
- PAN → auto-fetch name, DOB, address via DigiLocker integration; pre-fill form fields; user confirms, doesn't retype
- Manual video KYC fallback when AI facial recognition fails — AI failure should not mean account lock
- Single consolidated review screen before final submission

**P1 — Should ship**
- Aadhaar card scan via OCR (camera-scan the number like Amazon does for credit cards) to eliminate manual entry
- Per-step drop-off analytics (step name, exit rate, device/OS breakdown)
- Smart retry with context: "Your Aadhaar OTP expired — tap to resend" (not a generic error)

**P2 — Nice to have**
- Truecaller SDK for phone number pre-fill (reduces one entry step)
- Progress bar + step count to reduce perceived friction
- Resume-where-you-left-off for sessions abandoned mid-flow

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- OTP consolidation: user receives at most 2 OTPs total (phone + Aadhaar); email verify happens silently post-submission — *pass/fail: ≤2 OTPs per session.*
- Pre-fill: PAN entry triggers DigiLocker auto-fetch; name/DOB appear within 2s; user edits are respected — *pass/fail: fields populated, no retype required.*
- AI KYC fallback: when facial recognition fails, user is offered video KYC within the same session without restart — *pass/fail: zero forced restarts on AI failure.*
- Review screen: every submitted field visible on one screen before final CTA — *pass/fail: user can audit all data before submit.*

## Success Metrics

**North-star:** Account-opening completion rate (start → funded account). Baseline ~60%. Target ~75% in 90 days.

**Guardrails**
- KYC pass integrity / fraud-flag rate: no increase vs. baseline
- Manual video KYC usage rate: monitored (rising rate signals AI model degradation — escalate to ML team)
- Per-step drop-off: no individual step worsens
- Regulatory step omission rate: zero

## Wireframes / User Flows

**Flow: OTP consolidation + PAN pre-fill**  
`Phone entry → [DigiLocker fetch triggered] → fields pre-filled → user confirms → single OTP screen (phone + Aadhaar) → verified ✓ → proceed`

**Flow: AI KYC with fallback**  
`Facial verify step → [AI attempt] → success: proceed | failure: "Verification didn't work — try video KYC instead" → video KYC slot booked → proceed`

**Flow: Review screen**  
`All P0 steps complete → Review (name, DOB, PAN, Aadhaar last 4, address, photo) → Submit`

## Risks / Open Questions

- **Aadhaar OTP + phone OTP simultaneous:** UIDAI does not allow a single OTP to satisfy both — confirmed via API docs. "One OTP" is the phone OTP; Aadhaar remains separate. The consolidation win is UX sequencing, not literally one OTP total.
- **DigiLocker integration scope:** Does Jupiter/Federal Bank already have a DigiLocker agreement? If not, this is a compliance procurement task, not just engineering — check before committing to P0 timeline.
- **Name mismatch (PAN vs. Aadhaar):** Common for married name changes and transliteration differences. Flag mismatch, let user proceed with a note — do not hard-block.
- **Open:** What is the current AI KYC failure rate? Without this baseline, we can't set a meaningful target for fallback usage.

---

## Rollout Plan

**Phase 1 (Days 0–30):** 10% of new sign-ups. Instrument drop-off per step. Decision gate: if completion rate improves ≥8pp and fraud-flag rate is flat, proceed; if guardrails breach, rollback immediately.

**Phase 2 (Days 31–60):** 50% rollout. Activate Aadhaar OCR scan (P1). A/B test consolidated OTP vs. control.

**Phase 3 (Days 61–90):** 100% rollout. Evaluate P2 items (Truecaller, progress bar). Instrument video KYC fallback rate for ML team.
